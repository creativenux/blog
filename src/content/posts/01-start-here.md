## A short story

> When we are no longer able to change a situation, we are challenged to change ourselves - Victor Frankl 

It started in 2016 after I failed [JAMB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joint_Admissions_and_Matriculation_Board). Until then, failure wasn’t something I knew. That was the first time. The only thoughts in my head were that I’d let myself down and disappointed my parents. Instead of facing the failure and studying for the next exam, I distracted myself with online articles. One day, I stumbled onto programming and started researching how to become a programmer. I started learning [C](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C_(programming_language)) but couldn’t make sense of anything after a while. I wanted a small victory. Something I could see. Not just text in a command prompt. I read about web development - HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP and MySQL were the stack back then. And so my journey began.

## The Journey of a Thousand Miles

The first book I read was [HTML and CSS: Design and Build Websites by Jon Duckett](https://www.amazon.co.uk/HTML-CSS-Design-Build-Websites/dp/1118008189) and the introduction section made me fall in love with the web. I started my journey by building simple web pages and I still remember the joy on my face the day I finally figured out how to use the `<img>` tag to render an image correctly (I struggled for days to understand `./` and `../`). What started as curiosity quickly became a way of seeing the world, problems as puzzles, and code as the language to solve them while also preparing for my second JAMB exam. Study all day, code all night.

At some point, self-doubt set in. Questions crept in. What am I really learning? Does this even matter? I took the courage to ask a question on a blog where I read tutorials. I don’t remember the exact question, but I got a reply that changed my life. I learned to silent the self-doubts enough for me to keep going.

![Gmail Vision](/Gmail-Vision.png "Gmail Vision")
<p style="text-align: center;"><em>Email response I got from the owner of the blog where I asked the question</em></p>

After a couple of months building dummy projects and showing people what I was learning, I got an offer to build a computer based test software for a secondary school. This was my first major project. I was not sure I could pull it off, I had no idea how to start or even what to do.

> Whatever you can do or dream you can, begin it. Boldness has genius, power, and magic in it. - Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

I remember that the software demo was scheduled to be held on a Friday morning, while other features worked well, marking of questions after the candidate selected an option failed (it has never failed prior to this). I was sweating profusely. It was the first time my software failed. Not in private, but in front of people watching me present. I ended up spending all weekend trying to figure out what happened and how to fix it.

> Experience is the name everyone gives to their mistakes. 

## The Twist

What began as a distraction became a career where solving tough technical challenges feels most rewarding when the result makes life simpler for someone else.

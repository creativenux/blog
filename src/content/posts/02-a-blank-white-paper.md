I once tried a simple exercise. 

> Take a blank piece of white A4 paper and answer the question: What is your billionaire dream? 

Not how to become a billionaire. The reason you would want to be one.

The exercise came to my mind one afternoon after listening to [Billion Naira Dream](https://music.youtube.com/watch?v=_bO6JHKFdAo&si=5axdVtTfhCwYQOGG) by Brymo. For a while, the page stayed blank. The song made the idea simple but when you try to answer the question, it turns out harder than it looks.

The question feels less like a test of ambition and more like a test of clarity. It reminded me of the question in the 2009 movie [Exam](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1258197/). The kind of question that forces you to look inward.

Finally, I wrote two words: Freedom and Happiness.

## Freedom

I grew up noticing a pattern. 

Parents left home as early as 5 AM to beat Lagos traffic. A long workday followed. Then hours on the road again before they finally got home. By the time they returned home, they were tired. Weekends disappeared quickly and family time was usually Sunday breakfast or dinner. Vacations rarely happened unless illness forced one.

No one seemed to plan this life. Most people drifted into it. Very few escape it.

As a teenager, I made a quiet promise to myself: _I will never live like this_. At the time, I didn’t know what the alternative was. I only knew what I wanted to avoid.

Years later I realized what bothered me about that pattern. It was the lack of control over time. Most people trade their time for stability. The trade is so common that it stops looking like a trade. It starts looking like life itself.

But if you want freedom, you need control over time and choice.

### Time

I wanted control of my time. Waking up without alarms. Learning things out of curiosity. Taking random long walks that may or may not turn into an idea. Packing a bag and leaving for a trip without it feeling strange. Sitting with someone who has nothing in common with me and feeling genuinely curious.

### Choice

Escaping the pattern requires optionality. The ability to walk away from rooms that feel wrong and commit to the ones that feel right. To say yes to only the things that matter. To place long bets without asking permission. 

## Happiness 

For a long time I thought happiness came from outside 

- An achievement
- A title
- A partner
- External validation
- A profession
- Status or popularity

But the pattern I saw growing up made me question that assumption. From the outside it looked complete. Yet time was constantly traded for stability, curiosity replaced with obligation. It wasn’t unhappiness exactly, it looked more like conditioning. A life shaped by expectation rather than intention.

It’s 2026, ten years later. I tried the exercise again. 

One of the words changed. Freedom stayed, happiness didn’t. Not because it mattered less, but because it no longer needed to be chased and over time I started noticing people who chased happiness directly rarely seemed to find it. Happiness seems more like a default state. It appears when there is no external expectation.

So the second word became Stewardship.

## Stewardship

Freedom of the kind I imagined usually requires wealth. And wealth tends to come from solving a problem at scale.

The most interesting version of that is building something that improves everyday life without asking for attention. Something people quietly depend on the way they rely on electricity or clean water. An Infrastructure. 

If you build something genuinely useful, wealth can follow as a side effect. But once it does, the question changes. It becomes less about accumulation and more about responsibility.

Stewardship means building things that last. Systems that outlive the person who created them. Work that leaves the world a little more functional than it was before.

So my billionaire dream turned out to be two words.

Freedom and Stewardship.
